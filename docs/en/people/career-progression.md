## Individual Contributor Levels

### IC2: Junior (Associate/Entry)

**Career Stage**: Entry-level professional developing foundational skills

#### Expertise & Technical Depth

- Applies basic theories, principles, and concepts in specialized area
- Learns and applies primary techniques under guidance
- Continuously expands skill sets including company policies and industry knowledge
- Grows competency through structured tasks and mentorship
- **Focus**: 100% Operational

#### Scope & Decision Making

- Works on routine and basic tasks with clearly defined parameters
- Makes decisions about own work guided by well-defined options
- Refers non-standard matters to senior team members
- Contributes to one or more project components following general directions
- Work is closely supervised with regular review

#### Leadership & Influence

- Plans daily tasks with guidance
- Works with well-defined stakeholders within immediate team
- Influences through research and providing inputs
- Learns principles of effective communication and collaboration
- Responsible for individual assignments and deliverables

#### Complexity & Problem Solving

- Solves defined problems using established methods
- Problems require analysis of identifiable factors with clear solutions
- Follows documented procedures and best practices
- Applies knowledge and basic techniques to resolve routine issues
- Conveys straightforward technical information

#### Background

- Bachelor's degree + 1 year OR Master's degree
- Foundational knowledge in relevant field
- Demonstrated eagerness to learn and grow

**Typical Roles**: Junior Engineer, Associate Product Manager, Junior Designer

### IC3: Professional (Developing)

**Career Stage**: Developing professional with growing independence

#### Expertise & Technical Depth

- Develops and applies growing advanced techniques and principles
- Near-complete use of standard principles, theories, and concepts
- Expanding knowledge across related specialty areas
- Demonstrates increasing competency and autonomy
- **Focus**: 100% Operational

#### Scope & Decision Making

- Handles moderate complexity work with opportunity for review
- Makes decisions about own work and occasionally others' work within documented parameters
- Independently resolves routine and some non-routine issues
- Responsible for multiple project aspects, determining methods to achieve results
- Works under general supervision with independent judgment

#### Leadership & Influence

- Plans and schedules own work across multiple days
- Begins working with more varied and changing stakeholders
- Influences through recommending approaches and solutions
- Gains cooperation from others, exchanges technical information
- Responsible for multiple project aspects

#### Complexity & Problem Solving

- Problems require analysis of identifiable factors
- Determines course of action based on guidelines, modifies based on experience
- Handles escalated issues from junior team members
- Applies growing problem-solving sophistication
- Conveys technical information clearly to team

#### Background

- Bachelor's degree + 3 years OR Master's degree + 2 years OR PhD + 1 year
- Demonstrated growth in technical skills
- Track record of independent contributions

**Typical Roles**: Software Engineer, Product Manager, Designer

### IC4: Senior (Fully Functioning)

**Career Stage**: Fully functioning professional at career level

#### Expertise & Technical Depth

- Applies complete and practical knowledge across multiple specializations
- Demonstrates full competence and may be gaining exposure to more complex work
- Understands interrelationships between different disciplines
- Deep application of principles, theories, and concepts
- **Focus**: 100% Operational

#### Scope & Decision Making

- Handles moderate complexity with diverse issues requiring minimal review
- Makes independent decisions guided by experience and learned knowledge
- Regularly resolves routine and non-routine issues independently
- Contributes to significant project components, impacting own and others' work
- Works under broad guidelines with regular application of independent judgment

#### Leadership & Influence

- Plans own work within scheduled guidelines across projects
- Identifies and actively manages stakeholders
- Begins influencing work team products, processes, and results
- Presents results and recommendations across discipline
- Serves as Recommender and occasional Activator on decisions

#### Complexity & Problem Solving

- Problems are somewhat varied, require analysis of multiple factors
- Assesses unusual circumstances using sophisticated problem-solving techniques
- Identifies causes and builds solutions independently
- Provides solutions to issues in creative and effective ways
- Often recommends integration approaches across projects

#### Background

- Bachelor's degree + 4 years OR Master's degree + 3 years OR PhD + 2 years
- Proven track record of independent project delivery
- Strong technical and domain expertise

**Typical Roles**: Senior Engineer, Senior Product Manager, Senior Designer

### IC5: Staff (Seasoned/Expert)

**Career Stage**: Seasoned expert beginning to broaden beyond core area

#### Expertise & Technical Depth

- Applies advanced and wide-ranging knowledge across domain
- Combines emerging business acumen with technical depth
- Understands interrelationships between disciplines, serves as technical advisor
- Deep application of principles across most areas of specialization
- **Focus**: 80% Operational / 20% Strategic

#### Scope & Decision Making

- Handles complex work and issues with significant interdependencies
- Makes decisions on technical approaches, processes, occasionally ambiguous situations
- Independently resolves most complex issues, teams to solve ambiguous challenges
- Contributes to and often drives achievement of critical functional goals
- Collaborates as Recommender for systemic issues, occasionally Activator

#### Leadership & Influence

- Plans, schedules, and integrates tasks with limited direction across projects
- Begins directing application of existing principles
- Contributes to stakeholder management plans, develops relationships across BU
- Influences work team and serves as both Recommender and Activator
- Often regarded as in-house expert on specific areas

#### Complexity & Problem Solving

- Problems are varied, diverse, and often "hidden" or systemic
- Provides solutions to complex issues with creative insights from experience
- Works on high-impact projects and challenges
- Regularly guides others in solving highly complex issues
- Significant contributor and recommender for far-reaching challenges

#### Background

- Bachelor's degree + 6 years OR Master's degree + 4 years OR PhD + 4 years
- Recognized technical expert in specific domain
- Track record of complex project leadership

**Typical Roles**: Staff Engineer, Staff Product Manager, Staff Designer, Technical Lead

### IC6: Principal (Lead)

**Career Stage**: Lead individual contributor with cross-functional impact

#### Expertise & Technical Depth

- Applies advanced business acumen to technical domain
- Combines highly specialized field knowledge with broad expertise in related areas
- Deep expertise across multiple specializations and interdependent areas
- Begins building reputation as in-house expert across organization
- **Focus**: 70% Operational / 30% Strategic

#### Scope & Decision Making

- Handles highly complex work with significant interdependencies
- Makes decisions regarding technical approaches influencing others and organization
- Advises multiple areas on decision making in ambiguous situations
- Directs work contributing to critical functional and organizational goals
- Often serves as Recommender, occasionally Activator for systemic issues

#### Leadership & Influence

- Influences planning of others, regularly plans work of team members
- Directs application of existing principles, participates in developing new practices
- Owns portions of stakeholder management plans across BUs
- Influences department offerings and function-level impact
- Applies flexible leadership styles to influence results

#### Complexity & Problem Solving

- Problems are novel with some precedent, require significant ingenuity
- Provides, guides, and uses innovative methods to solve unusual problems
- Leads teams in solving complex, cross-discipline issues critical to company
- Provides insight and technical leadership promoting new ideas
- Leads implementation strategies for solutions

#### Background

- Bachelor's degree + 9 years OR Master's degree + 6 years OR PhD + 6 years
- Recognized authority internally as domain expert
- Proven impact across multiple teams and functions

**Typical Roles**: Principal Engineer, Principal Product Manager, Principal Designer

- Applies deep expertise and emerging technologies to drive innovation
- Leads teams in solving most highly complex challenges with limited precedent
- Pioneers development and sponsors implementation strategies
- Solutions require strategic judgment with limited information

#### Background

## Management Levels

### M4: Lead Manager

**Career Stage**: First-level management supervising individual contributors

#### Leadership & Scope

- Supervises team/group performing singular or closely related tasks
- Provides HR management responsibilities (hire, promote, assess)
- Directs day-to-day operations with weekly planning
- Often collaborates with other functional areas and outside vendors
- Assigns tasks, monitors work, exercises discretion in planning workload
- **Focus**: 80% Operational / 20% Strategic

#### Expertise & Technical Skills

- Mastery of work supervised and broad knowledge of related areas
- May become actively involved to meet schedules and resolve problems
- Applies advanced principles and strategies to multiple specialty areas
- Often serves as technical and process coach to build team bench strength
- Advises team on moderately complex matters

#### Decision Making & Impact

- Makes independent decisions on matters of diverse scope
- Decisions guided by policies, procedures, and business plans
- Regularly interprets policies, makes daily decisions within guidelines
- Implements plans to manage financials, business planning, resource management
- Influences through coaching and motivating team to action and results

#### Stakeholder Management & Influence

- Leads teams to engage and monitor needs of well-defined stakeholders
- Sets priorities for areas managed, including occasionally non-routine situations
- Influences through information sharing within closely related teams
- Provides input to building adaptable teams that influence outcomes
- Sets goals and reviews with focus on monthly priorities

#### Complexity & Problem Solving

- Leads teams to find solutions following established methodologies
- Interprets and resolves moderately complex issues requiring data analysis
- Follows established practices and procedures with basic analysis
- Begins providing fresh and practical solutions to operational challenges
- Regularly faced with unusual situations requiring policy interpretation

#### Background

- Bachelor's degree + 4 years OR Master's degree + 3 years OR PhD + 2 years
- Proven track record of technical excellence
- Demonstrated ability to lead and develop people

**Typical Roles**: Engineering Manager, Product Manager (with reports), Design Manager

### M5: Senior Manager

**Career Stage**: Middle management overseeing multiple sections or complex areas

#### Leadership & Scope

- Manages activities of multiple sections, complex work area, or sub-department
- Work managed is varied and may cross disciplines or include non-related groups
- Provides input to HR actions and department structure changes
- Guides staff by established function practices and well-defined procedures
- Monitors 6-12 month projects and programs
- **Focus**: 70% Operational / 30% Strategic

#### Expertise & Technical Skills

- Expertise in work managed, considerable business acumen
- Broad knowledge of related areas and cross-functional understanding
- Widely applies advanced principles, strategies, and concepts
- May perform escalated and more complex duties directly
- Leads teams to develop new methods, products, processes

#### Decision Making & Impact

- Leads teams in exploring options and making recommendations on large projects
- Makes independent decisions around diverse scope impacting delivery and resourcing
- Influences decisions in related areas and across department
- Implements plans on complex matters for financials, business planning, resources
- Accountable for financials, schedules, and resource usage with broad impact

#### Stakeholder Management & Influence

- Manages teams to assess basic stakeholder needs and contributes to tactics
- Monitors and assesses results, resetting stakeholder approaches as needed
- Influences through motivating within and across organization to produce results
- Provides input to building highly adaptable teams
- Regularly makes significant decisions directly impacting project success

#### Complexity & Problem Solving

- Largely same as M4 but begins managing teams solving problems with limited precedent
- Exposes and resolves moderately to complex issues crossing disciplines
- Presents results and recommendations across discipline
- Develops persuasive cases to educate and persuade stakeholders
- Works on issues requiring conceptual thinking balanced with practical solutions

#### Background

- Bachelor's degree + 6 years OR Master's degree + 4 years OR PhD + 4 years
- Proven success managing managers or large IC teams
- Track record of cross-functional leadership

**Typical Roles**: Senior Engineering Manager, Senior Product Manager (large team), Head of Design

### M6: Head of/Director

**Career Stage**: Senior management directing sub-function or small functional area

#### Leadership & Scope

- Manages activities of two or more sections, disciplines, or major processes
- Directs activities through lower-level management and/or vendors
- Often includes variety of work groups across disciplines with regional/global reach
- Accountable for HR actions and changes to department structure
- Provides input to department strategy and large visible projects (1-3 year horizon)
- **Focus**: 60% Operational / 40% Strategic

#### Expertise & Technical Skills

- Expertise in several specialized areas/disciplines
- Recognized authority for executives, plays key role in improving intellectual capital
- Performs portions of more complex, novel work directly with team assistance
- Builds knowledge through strategic hires, skill building, and methodology expertise
- Leads teams to develop new methods and solve company-wide matters

#### Decision Making & Impact

- Makes independent decisions around design/implementation of strategy for new programs
- Works with senior management to establish strategic plans and objectives
- Makes final decisions on approach to achievement and implementation
- Accountable for financials, schedules, resource usage with far-reaching business impact
- Establishes plans for major projects, accountable for budget

#### Stakeholder Management & Influence

- Manages teams to anticipate advanced/systemically challenging stakeholder needs
- Contributes to development/updating of strategy and associated plans
- Expands, strengthens, develops stakeholder relationships coaching team networks
- Influences actions and results through collaboration down management chain
- Often advocates, educates, and gains support for projects with top management

#### Complexity & Problem Solving

- Leads teams in solving complex, cross-discipline issues critical to company
- Translates business and technical strategy into functional plans
- Guides teams in execution to solve persistent challenges
- Works on issues where analysis requires conceptual thinking with practical solutions
- Develops persuasive and easy-to-understand cases for executive decisions

#### Background

- Bachelor's degree + 9 years OR Master's degree + 6 years OR PhD + 6 years
- Proven track record leading multiple teams
- Strong business acumen and strategic thinking

**Typical Roles**: Director of Engineering, Director of Product, Head of Design

### M7: VP (Vice President)

**Career Stage**: Top management with regional/global reach

#### Leadership & Scope

- Leads activities of one or more sub-functions/divisions with regional/global reach
- Directs through subordinate directors/managers across organization
- Accountable for HR actions and major organizational structure changes
- Oversees multi-year strategies and plans with top leaders
- Cascades strategy to functional plans across organization
- **Focus**: 50% Operational / 50% Strategic

#### Expertise & Technical Skills

- Expertise in several specialized areas/disciplines with broad business knowledge
- Recognized authority that plays key role in intellectual capital for organization
- Performs complex novel work, builds organizational knowledge
- Well-versed in internal and external methodologies
- Oversees teams to develop new methods and solve company-wide matters

#### Decision Making & Impact

- Develops and provides strategy, vision, and direction to multiple functions
- Implements company-wide policies and practices
- Leads teams to provide information and input to executive management
- Accountable for function financial results impacting overall performance
- Outcomes have far-reaching impact to business segment, products, and goals

#### Stakeholder Management & Influence

- Persuades others in bringing closure to sensitive, multi-faceted challenges
- Manages relationships with complex, cross-domain/functional stakeholder groups
- Removes/remedies significant impediments to meeting business/technology goals
- Influences through building cross-functional networks and strong followership
- Serves as top influencer/advocate for new, transformative ideas and projects

#### Complexity & Problem Solving

- Builds and guides teams in solving company's most highly complex challenges
- Builds and nurtures environment of innovation and creative problem solving
- Works with senior leadership to identify and assemble talent to solve problems
- Through assessment of intangible variables, identifies fundamental issues
- Anticipates factors influencing strategies and company's market position

#### Background

- Bachelor's degree + 12 years OR Master's degree + 8 years OR PhD + 6 years
- Proven track record of organizational leadership
- Strategic vision and executive presence

**Typical Roles**: VP of Engineering, VP of Product, VP of Design

## Switching Career Tracks

Team members can move between IC and Management tracks based on interests, strengths, and business needs. This flexibility allows everyone to find their optimal contribution path.

### IC to Management

**When to Consider**:

- Passionate about developing people and building teams
- Enjoy navigating organizational dynamics and stakeholder management
- Want to influence through others' work rather than individual contributions
- Strong leadership and communication skills

**Typical Path**: IC4/IC5 â†’ M4 (often with mentor/shadow period)

**Requirements**:

- Demonstrated technical excellence at current level
- Leadership examples: mentoring, project leadership, cross-team collaboration
- Manager endorsement and leadership interest discussion
- 12+ months in current role
- Business need for management position

### Management to IC

**When to Consider**:

- Miss hands-on technical work and individual contribution
- Prefer influence through expertise rather than organizational authority
- Want deeper specialization in technical domain
- Enjoy solving complex problems directly

**Typical Path**: M4/M5 â†’ IC5/IC6 (depending on tenure and expertise)

**Requirements**:

- Maintain technical currency alongside management responsibilities
- Discussed with manager as part of career planning
- Business need and appropriate IC role available
- Smooth team transition plan

!!! note "Track Switching Support"

    Career track changes are normal and supported. Discuss with your manager during quarterly reviews. We value both paths equally and compensate based on scope and impact, not track choice.

## Promotion Process

### Promotion Criteria

Promotions require consistent demonstration of next-level performance:

1. **Performance**: Meeting/exceeding current level expectations for 6+ months
2. **Next-Level Work**: Consistently operating at next level for 6-12 months
3. **Scope**: Taking on expanded scope and complexity appropriate to next level
4. **Impact**: Demonstrated impact on team, function, or company
5. **Readiness**: Manager and skip-level agreement on promotion readiness

### Promotion Timeline

- **Quarterly Reviews**: Discuss progress toward next level
- **Self-Nomination**: Indicate interest in promotion consideration
- **Manager Assessment**: Manager evaluates against criteria
- **Calibration**: Management team ensures consistency across organization
- **Decision & Communication**: Typically within 2-4 weeks of nomination
- **Effective Date**: Usually next quarter start, with compensation adjustment

### Promotion Package

Manager prepares promotion case including:

- **Performance Examples**: 3-5 specific examples demonstrating next-level impact
- **Scope Evidence**: Projects, decisions, and influence at next level
- **Peer Feedback**: Input from collaborators and stakeholders
- **Growth Trajectory**: Pattern of increasing impact over time
- **Business Impact**: Quantified results where possible

### Self-Advocacy Tips

- **Document Wins**: Keep running list of achievements and impact
- **Seek Feedback**: Regularly ask manager about readiness and gaps
- **Fill Gaps**: Proactively take on next-level work and responsibilities
- **Build Visibility**: Share work in demos, docs, and cross-team forums
- **Be Patient**: Promotions reward sustained performance, not single achievements

## Compensation & Levels

### Compensation Philosophy

Compensation reflects scope, impact, and market rates rather than tenure alone. Both IC and Management tracks offer equivalent compensation potential at similar scope levels.

**Compensation Components**:

- **Base Salary**: Market-competitive based on level, location, and role
- **Equity**: Meaningful ownership through stock options
- **Bonuses**: Performance-based for exceptional contributions
- **Benefits**: Comprehensive benefits package

### Leveling Factors

Compensation bands consider:

- **Geographic Location**: Adjusted for market rates and cost of living
- **Role Specialization**: Some roles (e.g., ML/AI, Security) command higher market rates
- **Performance**: High performers may be positioned higher in band
- **Experience**: Prior experience may influence initial placement

!!! info "Compensation Reviews"

    Compensation is reviewed quarterly during performance reviews. Adjustments consider individual performance, promotion eligibility, market changes, and company performance. Discuss compensation questions with your manager.

## Growth & Development

### Development Resources

**Learning Budget**: Annual allocation for courses, books, conferences, certifications (contact HR for current amounts)

**Internal Growth**:

- Quarterly 1:1s with manager for career planning
- Mentorship programs pairing with senior team members
- Internal tech talks, workshops, and knowledge sharing
- 10% time for exploring new technologies (with manager approval)

**External Growth**:

- Conference attendance for relevant industry events
- Online courses (Coursera, Udemy, Pluralsight, etc.)
- Certifications (AWS, GCP, Azure, product management, etc.)
- Books and technical publications

### Career Planning

**Quarterly Review Discussion Points**:

- Progress toward current level mastery
- Readiness for next level
- Specific skills or experiences needed
- Opportunities to demonstrate next-level work
- Career interests (IC vs. Management, technical areas)
- Learning and development goals

**Creating Your Growth Plan**:

1. **Self-assess**: Review current level criteria and identify strengths/gaps
2. **Identify Goals**: Choose 2-3 skills or experiences for next quarter
3. **Find Opportunities**: Work with manager to get exposure to next-level work
4. **Get Feedback**: Regularly check progress with manager and peers
5. **Document Impact**: Track achievements for promotion discussions
